Daily Living and Life Skills

Daily living and life skills are the skills that we need to live independently and to participate fully in our communities. They include a wide range of skills, such as:

  • ⁍ Personal care: bathing, dressing, grooming, toileting, and other self-care tasks.
  • ⁍ Mobility: getting around your home and community, including using public transportation or driving.
  • ⁍ Meal preparation and eating: planning, preparing, and eating meals.
  • ⁍Housekeeping and laundry: cleaning and maintaining your home, and doing your laundry.
  • ⁍ Medication management: taking your medications as prescribed.
  • ⁍ Communication: communicating with others, including using assistive technology.
  • ⁍ Socialization and recreation: participating in social activities and recreation.

Daily living and life skills are important for everyone, but they are especially important for people with disabilities. People with disabilities may need additional support to develop and maintain their daily living and life skills.

There are a number of ways to develop and maintain your daily living and life skills. You can take classes, join support groups, or work with a therapist or counselor. You can also ask your doctor or other healthcare provider for resources and referrals.

Here are some tips for developing and maintaining your daily living and life skills:

  • ⁍ Set realistic goals. Don't try to do too much at once. Start with one or two skills that you want to focus on.
  • ⁍ Break down tasks into smaller steps. This will make them seem less daunting.
  • ⁍ Ask for help when you need it. Don't be afraid to ask for help from your doctor, therapist, counselor, or other support people.
  • ⁍ Be patient. It takes time to develop and maintain new skills.

Developing and maintaining your daily living and life skills can help you to live a more independent and fulfilling life.
